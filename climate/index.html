Coastal Climate Change Assessment

Client: Government of British Columbia and Marine Plan Partnership for the North Pacific Coast.
Timeline: 2020 through 2022.

Led two regional assessments of climate change risk and developed adaptation approaches for marine and coastal sectors in support of a regional Coastal Management Strategy for British Columbia. Patrick was the principal investigator on the studies, providing oversight of the design, execution, and quality of deliverables. His role involved:

  • Compiling regionally downscaled climate projections for select marine and coastal variables.
  • Analyzing potential influence of future climate and environmental conditions on ecologial, social, and infrastructure components.
  • Producing maps and comprehensive study reports for use in policy development by the government.

Citation: Tamburello, N, J Eyzaguirre, R Hodgson, C Quirion, and P Burke. 2022. Marine Climate Change Assessment for the South Coast of British Columbia. Report prepared by ESSA Technologies Ltd. for the British Columbia Ministry of Land, Water and Resource Stewardship. Available online.

Deriving Bioclimatic Variables

Patrick generated 19 bioclimatic variables for climate change studies in Kenya, Ethiopia, and Tanzania using locally downscaled high-temporal resolution data for 20 ensembles.

Bioclimatic variables for East Africa

University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign.
Timeline: 2023.

Climate impact assessments and adaptation planning are challenged by the scale of available data in many regions of the world. Global Climate Model (GCM) outputs are too coarse for local and regional assessments. To support regional climate planning in East Africa, a 2.5M km2 region of high geographic diversity and high climate vulnerability, Patrick derived a set of bioclimatic variables from statistically downscaled daily temperature and precipitation data at 211 locations.

The updated dataset encnahces the value of the downscaled data for applications in agriculture, conservation, hydrology, and adaptation planning. For example, WorldClim variables are essential imputs in species distribution models, such as MaxEnt, and agriculatural yield models which inform climate adaptation planning.

Statistically downscaled data used in the analysis can be found here: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41597-019-0038-1.

Indigenous Food Security

Using traditional knowledge and climate models, Patrick worked with Tŝilhqot’in communities to plan for land stewardship activities by asking: will climate change alter where traditional food sources grow within Tŝilhqot’in territory?

Climate Change Modeling to Inform Food Security Management

Client: Tŝilhqot’in National Government.
Timeline: 2021 through 2022.

Patrick led a study to assess potential impacts of climate change on food security on 1,700 square kilometers of Tŝilhqot’in Nation lands (Nenqay Deni). His role involved:

  • Applying downscaled regional climate data from PRISM.
  • Modeled changes in distribution of vegetation communities using species distribution models with MaxEnt.

Patrick oversaw the development of public-facing materials, incuding an ArcGIS webmap, designed to communicate project results to community members in an accessible format. The webmap is available online.
